FOUNDATIONS


Foundations are a part of the substructure that sustains and transmits the load of the superstructure down to the ground under it. There are different types of loads transmitted from above: dead load, live load(imposed load), and wind loads. The major function of the foundation is to transmit these loads in such a way that there would'nt be any movement or settlement that would make the building unstable, or cause damage to other parts of the building. According to Newton's third law of motion, 'For every action, theres an qual and opposite reaction'. Similarly, the loads transmitted through the foundation have an equal and opposite reaction that resists the downward pressure. If the downward pressure is more than the ground's passive presseure(bearing capacity), settlement of the ground could occcur.
